= Me — The On-The-Fly Pedagogical Projection
:toc: left
:toclevels: 2

[IMPORTANT]
====
**This directory is a reserved slot for a future build, not a completed component.**
Me is *not* a pre-built compiler or dialect. It is a runtime agent-generated projection
over My-Lang, assembled on demand by the aspect-agents in the kin framework, per learner,
per task, from an A2ML task spec. This README describes the design; the generator itself
is built in a subsequent session.
====

== What Me Actually Is

Me is **not a dialect** in the normal sense. It does not pre-exist as a compiler, parser, or
language. It is generated per-learner, per-task, on demand, by the aspect-agents in the
`tentacles-agentic-syllabus` framework, from an A2ML task spec that encodes both the goal
and the task constraints.

Me is always a **projection** of real My-Lang (never a divergent fork), parameterised by a
chosen base dialect and a set of inclusions, exclusions, and constraints configured by the
teacher or the learner.

== The Flow

. A learner (or teacher on behalf of learners) goes to the tentacles-agentic-syllabus
  framework and states:
+
* a **goal** (what they want to understand or accomplish),
* a **task** (the concrete thing they'll build),
* a **base dialect** (one of Solo, Duet, or Ensemble — see containment below),
* **inclusions, exclusions, and constraints** that shape what's in and out of scope.
+
. The spec is captured as an A2ML file alongside the relevant syllabus entry.
. The aspect-agents read the A2ML spec and **assemble a subset teaching & learning projection**
  (the "Me" for this task) for this learner.
. The learner works within that scoped projection. Attention stays focused; they cannot
  wander across the whole Turing-complete language accidentally.
. Me is **mutable during the exercise**: the learner can add features going up (if frustrated
  or curious) or tighten constraints further (for more challenge). The projection expands
  or contracts as they explore.

== The Dialect Containment My Projects Over

My-Lang's three real dialects are **nested subsets**, not parallel alternatives:

[source]
----
Solo  ⊂  Duet  ⊂  Ensemble
----

* **Solo** — simplest subset, innermost kernel.
* **Duet** — contains all of Solo plus additional features.
* **Ensemble** — contains all of Duet (and therefore all of Solo) plus additional features
  on top. Ensemble is effectively the full language.

When a learner picks a base dialect for their task, they are picking a **cut-off point** in
the containment hierarchy. Me then layers further constraints on top.

Me sits **outside** the containment hierarchy. It is the projector, not a dialect.

== Constraint-Based Pedagogy — The Real Payload

Me is not just "a smaller subset for beginners". Its pedagogical power is that a teacher
(or learner) can **ban specific features to force discovery of alternatives**.

The canonical example:

____
"Make a loop, but with `for`, `while`, and iterators banned from core syntax."
____

This forces the learner to invent recursion from first principles — they cannot just reach
for the obvious construct because Me has projected it out of their reach for this task.

Other patterns the same mechanism enables:

[cols="1,2",options="header"]
|===
| Ban                  | What the learner discovers
| mutation             | Immutability + functional pipelines
| named functions      | Anonymous composition + higher-order reasoning
| explicit types       | Type inference (or, when required, type-directed programming)
| aliasing             | Linear / affine discipline
| early return         | Expression-oriented control flow
| the target data structure | Reconstruction from primitives
|===

**The "what's banned" is first-class content in the A2ML task spec, not an afterthought.**
A teacher configures exclusions as a pedagogical lever, not as mere simplification. The
learner is always free to remove their own constraints if they want to explore further, but
the exercise's learning outcome depends on the constraint holding while they solve the task.

== Audience

Me is designed to serve everyone from an 8-year-old discovering `if` statements to a senior
professor exploring graded modal types — on the same infrastructure. The projection adapts
to the learner's chosen constraint set; there is no separate "beginner" and "expert" system.

The underlying insight is that the interesting difficulty of a task comes from the
*constraints*, not from the *size of the language*, and Me makes that difficulty
teacher-configurable.

== Example Task Spec (Illustrative — A2ML Schema TBD)

[source]
----
(me-task
  (goal "Build the tiniest compiler in the world")
  (task "Lex, parse, and evaluate a single-expression arithmetic language")
  (base-dialect duet)
  (include (recursion pattern-matching algebraic-data-types))
  (exclude (iteration mutation ensemble-only-features))
  (constraints
    (max-lines 200)
    (no-external-dependencies)
    (must-terminate))
  (learning-outcome
    "Discover that tree-walking recursion + algebraic data types
     is enough to build a compiler kernel."))
----

This spec is read by the aspect-agents, which then assemble a Me projection that exposes
exactly the features needed and hides everything else. The learner works inside that view.

== Why This Design (Rather Than a Static "Me Compiler")

Two earlier attempts to implement Me as a standalone static dialect existed in the repo
tree before this design was settled:

* `nextgen-languages/me-dialect/` (standalone submodule, ~1,487 LOC)
* `nextgen-languages/my-lang/dialects/me/` (subdirectory inside my-lang, ~2,619 LOC)

Both attempts treated Me as **a separate compiler for a fixed subset**. That mismatches the
real pedagogical need, which is:

* **Per-task** — the subset is not fixed; it depends on what's being taught.
* **Per-learner** — the same task can have different constraints for different learners.
* **Mutable mid-exercise** — the learner can scale up or tighten as they work.
* **Safe by construction** — because Me is always a projection of real My-Lang, it can never
  drift into a different language with different semantics.

A static compiler cannot meet those properties. An agent-driven runtime projector can.

The two earlier directories are being archived / sidelined (see their respective repos and
the `.machine_readable/` history) and are not being migrated into this slot. The real Me is
built fresh here, on top of the aspect-agent and kin-framework machinery that already lives
in `../agents/` and `../curriculum/`.

== Relationship to Other Parts of This Repo

* `../agents/` — the aspect-agents that assemble Me from the A2ML task spec.
* `../curriculum/` — the syllabus material whose entries reference Me task specs.
* `../docs/kin-framework.adoc` (to be written) — how aspect-agents coordinate to produce
  the projection.
* `../contractiles/` — the contractile definitions that constrain how Me projections behave
  under ADJUST / INTENT / MUST / TRUST discipline.

== Relationship to My-Lang

Me **consumes** My-Lang (specifically, projects over Solo/Duet/Ensemble). It does not own
or modify My-Lang itself. My-Lang refers to Me only as a pedagogical pointer — the canonical
reference to "how do learners engage with the language" lives here, not in the language repo.

See `nextgen-languages/my-lang/README` for the language-side pointer, and
`nextgen-languages/my-lang/frontier-practices/` for my-lang-specific applied learner
materials that consume this framework.
